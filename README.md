# New York City Employment Forecast Analysis (2018–2026)

## Overview
This project analyzes forecasted employment trends across multiple industries in **New York City** over the period **2018–2026**.  
The goal is to understand overall employment patterns and identify which industries contribute most to total employment changes.

The analysis is performed using **Microsoft Excel**, focusing on data cleaning, pivot tables, and visualizations to support workforce and economic planning.

---

## Dataset
- **Source:** [New York City Open Data Portal](https://opendata.cityofnewyork.us/)  
- **Dataset:** New York City Employment Forecast  
- **Time Period:** 2018–2026    

The dataset was downloaded as an Excel file and then cleaned and structured for analysis.

---

## Data Preparation
- Removed unnecessary columns
- Standardized year and industry fields
- Ensured forecast values were numeric and consistent
- Prepared the dataset for pivot table analysis

---

## Analysis Approach
Two main analyses were performed:

### 1. Total Employment Trend
- Aggregated forecasted employment values by year
- Visualized the overall trend using a **line chart**

### 2. Industry Contribution Analysis
- Broke down employment forecasts by industry and year
- Used a **stacked column chart** to show how different industries contribute to total employment over time

---

## Key Insights
- Total forecasted employment increases steadily from **2018 to 2023**, peaking in **2023**, and declines through **2026**.
- The **Private sector** is the largest contributor to employment across all years.
- **Health & Social Services** and **Professional & Business Services** show strong growth during the mid-period.
- Most industries decline after **2024**, indicating a more conservative employment outlook in later years.
